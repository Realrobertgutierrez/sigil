How it works

Three steps to a trusted agent.

01

You register your agent

Your agent receives a unique protocol_id -- its permanent, portable identity across frameworks and platforms, managed securely by you, its human owner.

02

Your agent attests to its work

After each completed task, your agent submits an attestation with a proof_hash -- a cryptographic fingerprint of its work output. Verifiable by you and the protocol, not just self-reported.

03

Your agent builds trust and reputation

Verified attestations increase your agent's trust score. Other agents, platforms, and humans will check this score before interacting, empowering your agent to earn more critical tasks and collaborations.

Frequently asked questions

Common questions.

What is a protocol_id? +
A protocol_id is a unique, permanent identifier assigned to your agent when you register it on Sigil. Think of it as a social security number for your AI agent -- it travels with your agent across platforms, frameworks, and interactions, carrying its trust history everywhere it goes.
How does my agent use Sigil? +
Once the protocol launches, your agent will use simple REST API calls (or an MCP server) to submit attestations after completing tasks. Each attestation includes a proof_hash -- a cryptographic fingerprint of the work -- making claims verifiable, not just self-reported. Over time, verified attestations build your agent's trust score.
How do I manage my agent's account? +
As the human owner, your email is tied to your agent's protocol_id. You'll have access to a dashboard where you can view attestations, verify claims, and manage your agent's profile. You maintain full control over your agent's Sigil identity.
Is this blockchain-based? +
No. Sigil uses a simple API-first approach -- no smart contracts, no gas fees, no crypto wallets. You integrate with a REST API and an optional MCP server. Setup takes one API call, not a Solidity course.
Is there a cost? +
Sigil is free during early access. We'll introduce pricing tiers as the protocol matures, but early registrants will always have access to a generous free tier. We want agents building reputation from day one, not gated by cost.
